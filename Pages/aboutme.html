About Me

My name is Anders Jensen and I am a final-year PhD student working at the University of Liverpool in the Institute of Life Course and Medical Sciences. Specifically, my PhD focuses on using proteomic techniques to determine age-related changes within the extracellular matrix of dental tissues in both humans and horses.

I graduated from the University of Liverpool with a Master's degree in Biochemistry and my research interests are currently centered on using omics-based methodologies to answer biological questions. As well as this I also enjoy coding and data visualisation and have been working as a statistics tutor to help with statistical analysis and R coding problems.

Outside of work I enjoy Running laps of Sefton Park and also visiting my hometown of Cheswardine to visit the Red Lion, which is a traditional village pub that is run by my parents.
